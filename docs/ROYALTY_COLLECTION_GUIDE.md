## 1. Understanding Royalty Types

There are several distinct types of music royalties, each collected through different organizations and mechanisms. Understanding these is critical to ensuring no revenue is left uncollected.

| Royalty Type | What Triggers It | Who Collects | Typical Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Performance** | Song played on radio, TV, live venue, or public space | BMI, ASCAP, SESAC | $0.06–$0.12 per play (radio) |
| **Digital Performance** | Song played on non-interactive digital radio (Pandora, SiriusXM, internet radio) | SoundExchange | $0.0018–$0.0025 per play |
| **Streaming** | Song streamed on interactive platforms (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music) | Distributor (DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby) | $0.003–$0.005 per stream |
| **Mechanical** | Song reproduced (downloaded, pressed to CD/vinyl, or streamed) | HFA / Harry Fox Agency | $0.091 per reproduction (statutory) |
| **Sync** | Song used in film, TV show, commercial, video game | Negotiated directly or via publisher | $500–$500,000+ per placement |
| **Print** | Sheet music sold | Publisher | 10–15% of retail price |

## 3. Digital Performance Royalties via SoundExchange

**SoundExchange** collects royalties for non-interactive digital transmissions — this includes internet radio stations, satellite radio (SiriusXM), and cable music channels.

### Why This Matters for RRB

When the RRB 7-channel broadcast system streams music over the internet, those streams qualify as non-interactive digital transmissions. SoundExchange collects royalties on behalf of both the **sound recording copyright owner** (typically the label or artist) and the **featured artist**.

### Registration Steps

1. **Register as a sound recording copyright owner** — Canryn Production Inc. should register with SoundExchange at [soundexchange.com](https://www.soundexchange.com).
2. **Register as a featured artist** — Seabrun Candy Hunter's estate should be registered as the featured artist.
3. **Provide ISRC codes** — Each recording needs an International Standard Recording Code (ISRC). If recordings don't have ISRCs, apply through the ISRC Registrant at [usisrc.org](https://usisrc.org).
4. **Submit repertoire** — Upload the catalog of recordings to SoundExchange's database.

### SoundExchange Payment Split

SoundExchange distributes royalties as follows:
- **45%** to the featured artist (Seabrun Candy Hunter's estate)
- **50%** to the sound recording copyright owner (Canryn Production Inc.)
- **5%** to non-featured artists (session musicians, if any)

## 5. Mechanical Royalties

Mechanical royalties are generated every time a song is **reproduced** — whether as a physical copy (CD, vinyl), a digital download, or an interactive stream. The current U.S. statutory mechanical rate is **$0.091 per song** for physical/download reproductions.

### Collection Methods

1. **Through your distributor** — Most digital distributors (DistroKid, TuneCore) handle mechanical royalty collection from streaming platforms automatically.
2. **Harry Fox Agency (HFA)** — For physical reproductions and additional mechanical royalty collection, register with HFA at [harryfox.com](https://www.harryfox.com).
3. **Mechanical Licensing Collective (MLC)** — For U.S. interactive streaming mechanicals, register at [themlc.com](https://www.themlc.com). This is required by the Music Modernization Act.

### Action Items

1. **Register with The MLC** — This ensures mechanical royalties from Spotify, Apple Music, etc. are collected.
2. **Register with HFA** — For broader mechanical royalty administration.
3. **Track physical sales** — If CDs or vinyl are sold through the merchandise shop, mechanical royalties apply.

## 6. Sync Licensing Royalties

Sync (synchronization) licensing generates royalties when music is used alongside visual media — films, TV shows, commercials, video games, and online content.

### How to Set Up

1. **Register with a sync licensing agency** — Consider Musicbed, Artlist, or Songtradr for catalog placement.
2. **Submit to music supervisors** — Build relationships with film/TV music supervisors who place music in productions.
3. **Set licensing terms** — Define standard rates for different use types (background, featured, title sequence).
4. **Maintain clean metadata** — Ensure all songs have clear ownership, publishing info, and contact details.

### Sync Revenue Potential

Sync placements can generate significant one-time fees plus ongoing performance royalties (collected by BMI) whenever the content airs.

## 7. Setting Up Broadcast Channels for Royalty Collection

The RRB ecosystem operates 7 broadcast channels. To ensure these generate royalties:

### For Internet Radio Streams (SoundExchange)

1. **Obtain a statutory license** — Internet radio stations in the U.S. operate under a statutory license. Register with SoundExchange as a webcaster.
2. **Report playlists** — SoundExchange requires webcasters to submit detailed playlists (census or sample reporting). The RRB content scheduler already tracks what plays on each channel.
3. **Pay webcaster royalties** — As a webcaster, Canryn Production pays a per-performance royalty to SoundExchange, which then distributes to artists and labels. Since Canryn owns the recordings, a significant portion comes back.

### For BMI Performance Tracking

1. **Register as a licensed venue/broadcaster** — BMI offers blanket licenses for internet radio. Ensure the RRB broadcast operation has a BMI license.
2. **Submit program logs** — Provide BMI with logs of what songs play on each channel. The QUMUS content scheduler generates these automatically.

### Automated Reporting via QUMUS
